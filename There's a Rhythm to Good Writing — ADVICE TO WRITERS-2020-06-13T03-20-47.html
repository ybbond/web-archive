    Quote of the Day

    There's a Rhythm to Good Writing

    May 24, 2016/

    I’ve tried to figure out what good writing is. I know it when I read it in other people’s work or my own. The closest I’ve come is that there’s a rhythm to the writing, in the sentence and the paragraph. When the rhythm’s off, it’s hard to read the thing. It’s a lot like music in that sense; there’s an internal rhythm that does the work of reading for you. It almost reads itself. That’s one of the things that’s hard to teach to people. If you don’t hear music, you’re never going to hear it. That internal rhythm in a sentence or a paragraph, that’s the DNA of writing. That’s what good writing is.

    SEBASTAIN JUNGER

    Junger's comments are truly poignant however he doesn't mention what I've discovered over the last 30 years of writing: that the ability to recognize rhythmic writing - both in our own as well as in others' is progressive, maturing as we do. Recently, I wrote an essay entitled: Why Do I Cook and in it I mention that like my writing, cooking is - by its very nature, progressive.

    I wrote this essay 5 years ago when, at the time I believed it contained a wonderful internal rhythm. It became buried in my "think about it" folder. This past month upon rediscovering it, I also reworked it and was horrified to realize in my current view it's lacking in true rhythmic flow. So what changed? Only me and my progressing in my writing quest.

    I liken the process to re-visiting a burger joint that, in our younger days, we knew to a certainty had the best burgers and fried onions ever. Upon a more current visit we're generally inclined to exclaim in dismay: "Wow, it's no where as good as I remember it!" This is what our progressing though our lives and our writing is all about.
